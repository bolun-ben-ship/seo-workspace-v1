# RightClick:AI — Agency Strategy

_Last updated: 2026-03-22_

---

## What RightClick:AI Actually Is

A high-leverage AI SEO service built for **white-label distribution through partners** — not direct client relationships. Partners (SEO agencies, web studios, growth consultants) resell the service under their own brand. Ben builds and maintains the capability; partners own the client relationships.

---

## Core Distribution Model

**White-label B2B2C:**
- Partners have existing client relationships and trust
- RightClick:AI is invisible to end clients — the work is the partner's
- Ben talks to partners, not clients. Partners talk to clients.
- Clients drain energy. Partners create leverage.

**Service bundling (two entry points):**
1. **SEO first** → AI SEO audit + monthly execution → upsell AI website rebuild
2. **Website first** → AI-assisted website build → upsell monthly SEO retainer

Both directions are valid. Partners choose entry point based on client need.

---

## Strategic Priorities

### 1. Build leverage-maximising systems
Every workflow must run with minimal Ben-hours per client. Skills, orchestrators, and automations are the product — not consultancy hours. The goal: one person servicing 20+ clients via partners with near-zero marginal effort per client.

### 2. Partner acquisition over client acquisition
Time spent finding and onboarding good partners > time spent on any individual client.
Target partners: SEO agencies (5–50 clients), Webflow studios, growth consultants.
Ideal partner: has recurring client relationships, wants to add AI execution without hiring.

### 3. Monthly recurring revenue via retainer model
One-off audits are lead-gen, not the business. The business is monthly retainers:
- Monthly SEO run (audit + content + on-page changes + report) per client
- Partners pay per seat or per client under management

### 4. Workflow completeness before scale
The system needs to be reliable before pitching to partners at volume.
Current gate: monthly-seo-run skill (audit → keywords → plan → blogs → post → report) working end-to-end for both Shopline and Webflow clients.

---

## What Success Looks Like

- 5+ active white-label partners within 6 months
- 20+ client sites under management (via partners)
- Monthly SEO run fully automated — one command, full report, zero manual steps
- Ben's time: building skills + partner calls only. No client calls.

---

## Key Decisions

**Partners vs direct:** Committed to partner-only model. Even if a client approaches directly, redirect to a partner or decline.

**Pricing structure:** To be determined with first partner. Starting anchor: per-client-per-month retainer that leaves margin for the partner to mark up.

**Tech stack boundary:** Shopline + Webflow for now. WordPress when there's enough partner demand to justify building the orchestrator.
