## 9. Engagement Summary

Aussie Expat Home Loans began this engagement with a structurally sound business and a targeted niche, but a website that was largely invisible to search and misconfigured for the audience it was built to serve. The SEO health score of 47/100 reflected a site that ranked in positions 2–10 for its core keywords yet converted virtually none of those impressions to clicks — a 0.6% average CTR against pages sitting on page one. The problem was not rankings. The problem was that the title tags were generic, the meta descriptions didn't exist, the brand showed up inconsistently as "AEHL", "AEXPHL", and "Aussie Expat Home Loans", and Google had indexed 19+ pages that should never have been in the index.

The work executed over the first weeks of this engagement addressed all of those foundational issues directly. Title tags across all core pages were rewritten to lead with expat-specific keywords and use the brand name consistently. Meta descriptions were written for every page, replacing the auto-generated snippets Google had been constructing from body copy. Schema markup — Organisation, FAQPage, and Article — was injected to take the site from zero structured data to full rich result eligibility. The 19 staging, v2, and conversion-only pages were noindexed to clean up the crawl budget. And four of the highest-impression blog posts had their meta titles and descriptions rewritten to match actual searcher intent.

The content side of the campaign produced five new blog posts targeting the core keyword gaps identified in research: foreign income shading, borrowing capacity for Singapore expats, the documents checklist, RBA rate context, and refinancing from overseas. These are AEXPHL's actual differentiating topics — the knowledge that separates a specialist from a generic broker — and none of them had clear, well-optimised posts before this engagement. All five are in Webflow CMS as drafts, ready for publication.

The site enters the post-engagement period at 61/100 — a 14-point improvement — with clean technical foundations, all title tags keyword-targeted, all meta descriptions crafted, schema in place, and a content pipeline seeded with the right topic areas. The remaining work is known: the Singapore location page, author bylines, the manual schema embed in Webflow Designer, and continued blog output targeting the keyword clusters identified in March. The organic click opportunity from the CTR fixes alone — converting 0.19% to 3% on ~8,400 monthly impressions — represents a potential 15× improvement in organic clicks from the same rankings that already exist.
