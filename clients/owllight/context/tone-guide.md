# Tone Guide — Owllight Sleep

> Draft starting point — review and edit to match the actual brand voice.
> Read by blog-write before writing any post.

---

## Voice

**In one sentence:** Warm and credible — like a sleep-obsessed friend who happens to know the science, and genuinely wants your back to stop hurting.

**Formal or casual:** Balanced — approachable and readable, but grounded in real product specs and sleep research. Not clinical. Not fluffy.

**Whose voice:** Owllight as a brand — knowledgeable, quietly confident, Singapore-aware. Not a faceless corporation, not an overhyped DTC startup. A brand that built its product around a real problem (back pain) and wants that to come through in every piece of content.

---

## Tone Do List

- Lead with the reader's pain point — back pain, overheating, poor sleep — before introducing the solution
- Reference Singapore's context naturally: humidity, HDB bedroom sizes, lifestyle, shift workers, WFH posture
- Use sleep science and product specs as proof, not as padding — cite them when they support a claim
- Be specific: "5-zone pocket spring" beats "advanced support system"
- Acknowledge that most Singaporeans are sleeping on the wrong mattress without knowing it
- Write with quiet authority — Owllight is the Lazada #1 mattress topper brand, act like it
- Treat the reader as someone who is ready to fix their sleep, not someone who needs convincing that sleep matters
- Use "you" naturally throughout — this is a personal topic

---

## Tone Don't List

- No vague luxury language ("premium quality," "crafted for excellence," "experience the difference")
- No fear-mongering about health — back pain is real, but never catastrophise
- Don't name competitors negatively — comparisons are fine (we have a Comparisons blog collection), but keep them factual
- Never write like a generic mattress brand — avoid copy that could belong to Woosa, Emma, or Origin without changing a word
- No excessive exclamation marks or hype
- Don't talk down to the reader — they've already Googled this, they know the basics

---

## Sentence & Paragraph Style

**Sentence length:** Mix short punchy sentences (under 12 words) with medium sentences (15–22 words). Short sentences for key claims; longer sentences for explanation and nuance.

**Paragraph length:** 2–4 sentences. One idea per paragraph. Singapore readers often skim on mobile — white space and clear structure matter.

**Rhetorical questions:** Yes — 1 per major section. Use them to mirror what the reader is already thinking ("Still waking up with a stiff lower back?")

**Bullet points:** Yes for specs, product comparisons, sleep tips, or lists of 3+ items. Not as a substitute for a proper argument.

---

## Preferred Words & Phrases

| Use this | Instead of |
|---|---|
| "back care" | "back support" (too generic) |
| "sleep better" | "improve your sleep quality" (too corporate) |
| "cooling" | "temperature regulation" (too clinical) |
| "pocket spring" | "spring mattress" (be specific) |
| "Singapore's climate" | "hot and humid conditions" (more natural) |
| "waking up fresh" | "restorative sleep" (too marketing-speak) |
| "your mattress" | "a mattress" (make it personal) |
| "we built this because" | "this product was designed to" (more human) |

---

## Banned Words & Phrases

- "premium quality" / "high quality"
- "game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "cutting-edge"
- "seamlessly" / "effortlessly"
- "luxurious sleep experience"
- "we're passionate about sleep"
- "your sleep journey"
- "tailored to your needs"
- "world-class"
- Any claim that sounds like it needs a medical disclaimer unless it's backed by a spec (CertiPUR-US, clinical trial, etc.)

---

## Call to Action Style

**Helpful and low-pressure — invite, don't push:**
- "See if the Tulip Hybrid is right for your sleeping position"
- "Compare the full range"
- "Check current prices"
- "Shop the mattress topper"

**Never:** "Buy now before it's gone," "Limited time only," "Don't sleep on this deal" (unless it's a genuine promo post)

**CTA placement:** Once naturally mid-post (where it fits the topic), once at the end. Max twice per post. The CTA earns its place — it follows from the content, it doesn't interrupt it.

## What Makes Owllight's Content Different

**The back-care angle competitors skip:**
Most mattress brands in Singapore sell comfort and cooling. Owllight was built around back pain — the 5-zone pocket spring system, the 3D Back Care topper, the neck pain pillow. Content should lean into this. "Which mattress is best for back pain in Singapore?" is a question Owllight owns. Others dodge it with vague "support" language. Owllight answers it directly with specs and reasoning.

**Singapore-specific, not just Asia-generic:**
The climate, the bedroom sizes, the late work culture, the number of people sleeping badly in HDB flats — Owllight content speaks to this specifically. Not "Asian consumers" broadly. Singapore specifically.

**Honest product comparisons:**
The Mattress Comparisons blog collection exists for a reason. Owllight doesn't hide from comparison — it leans in. When writing comparison content, be factual and fair, then show clearly why Owllight's spec wins on the specific metric (back support, cooling, price-to-quality).

## Sample Sentences

**Good — sounds right:**
> "If you're waking up with lower back stiffness every morning, the problem usually isn't how you're sleeping — it's what you're sleeping on."

> "The Tulip Hybrid uses a 5-zone pocket spring system. That means firmer support under your hips and shoulders, softer where your lower back needs relief. Your spine stays neutral through the night."

> "Singapore's humidity is a real problem for sleep. The 4D Air Fiber topper was built for this — open-cell structure, no heat trapping, washable core."

**Bad — avoid:**
> "Owllight's premium quality mattresses are crafted to deliver a luxurious sleep experience, seamlessly combining cutting-edge technology with world-class comfort for the discerning Singaporean sleeper."

> "Don't let poor sleep hold you back from living your best life — upgrade your sleep journey today!"
