There are four features that determine whether a mattress helps or hurts your back:

How to Choose the Right Mattress Firmness

Medium-firm (6–7 out of 10) is the clinically validated starting point for most Singapore adults. A 2025 study (PMC12071755) found that medium-firm mattresses produced the best sleep architecture scores across all sleeping positions. It supports spinal neutrality without creating excessive pressure at the hips and shoulders.

Here's the quick-reference guide by sleep position:

Sleep Position Recommended Firmness Why
Side sleeper Medium (5–6/10) Hip and shoulder pressure relief prevents nerve pain
Back sleeper Medium-firm (6–7/10) Lumbar support without hyperextension
Stomach sleeper Firm (7–8/10) Prevents lumbar sinking and spinal twist
Combination sleeper Medium-firm (6–7/10) Best average across positions

Body weight matters too. Lighter bodies (under 65kg) can often go one level softer than these guidelines suggest — you don't sink as deeply, so less firmness is needed to maintain alignment. Heavier bodies (over 90kg) should consider firmer options to prevent the "hammock effect" where hips sink below shoulder level.

The common belief that harder equals better for back pain is wrong. Excessive firmness creates pressure points at the hips and shoulders, which forces surrounding muscles to compensate — often worsening pain. The 55% reduction in back pain observed in the 2021 PMC systematic review was achieved with medium-firm, not firm.
