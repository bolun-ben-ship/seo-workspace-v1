## 2. Technical SEO

**Score: 35 / 100**

### 2.1 Crawlability & Indexation

| Issue | Severity | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| No `meta robots` directives on any page | 🟡 Medium | Pages are indexed by default, but there is no mechanism to prevent accidental indexation of thin or duplicate pages |
| `/search` disallowed in `robots.txt` for `User-agent: *` | 🟡 Medium | The homepage FAQ links to `/search`. Most bots (including AI crawlers) cannot follow this link. Googlebot is exempt via a blank `Disallow` rule, but it is non-standard and fragile |
| Orphan products in sitemap with no navigation | 🟡 Medium | `/products/11`, `/products/cloud-wrap`, and `/products/mattress-180-*` appear in the sitemap but have no internal links from navigation or category pages — they will not accumulate PageRank |
| Common paths redirect incorrectly | 🟡 Medium | `/about` → homepage (301), `/contact` → homepage (301), `/faq` → homepage (301), `/products` → `/collections` (301). Users and bots expecting standard URL conventions will be silently redirected. |
| Canonical trailing-slash inconsistency | 🟢 Low | Homepage canonical is `https://owllight-sleep.com` (no trailing slash) but the served URL is `https://owllight-sleep.com/` — a minor self-referencing inconsistency that can create confusion in Search Console |

### 2.2 Sitemap

The sitemap is structurally well-organised with 16 child sitemaps covering 4 locale variants (default, `en-ar`, `ar-ar`, and likely `en-sg` or similar) across 4 content types. However:

- Arabic locale variants are declared in the sitemap, implying international targeting, but **no `hreflang` tags are implemented on any page** (see Section 2.3).
- Orphan URLs are listed in the sitemap with no corresponding internal link structure.
- The typo URL `/pages/mattress-trial-singapore-100-nighs` ("nighs" instead of "nights") is confirmed indexed in the sitemap and **cannot be quietly fixed** — it will require a 301 redirect to a corrected URL plus sitemap update.

### 2.3 International SEO (hreflang)

🔴 **Critical gap.** The sitemap declares Arabic locale pages (`/ar-ar/`, `/en-ar/`) — a clear signal of Middle East market expansion — but **not a single `hreflang` tag exists on any page**. This means:

- Google cannot understand the relationship between the English and Arabic versions of any page.
- The Arabic pages may be treated as duplicate content rather than targeted regional variants.
- Search results in the Middle East will not serve the correct locale page.

**Required implementation:** Add `hreflang` `<link>` elements (or HTTP header equivalents) to all locale variants for each page, including a self-referencing tag and an `x-default` fallback.

### 2.4 Mobile Usability

🟠 **High.** The viewport meta tag sitewide reads:

```
maximum-scale=1.0, user-scalable=no
```

This **disables user zoom** across the entire site. It is:
- A violation of WCAG 1.4.4 (Resize Text, Level AA)
- A negative accessibility signal that Google has explicitly stated it considers in mobile usability assessments
- Harmful to users with visual impairments or those using assistive technologies

**Fix:** Remove `user-scalable=no` and `maximum-scale=1.0` from the global viewport meta tag. At minimum, set `maximum-scale=5.0`.

## 5. Schema & Structured Data

**Score: 28 / 100**

### 5.1 Critical Schema Errors

#### JSON-LD Parse Errors on All Blog Article Pages

🔴 **Critical.** All 6 blog article pages have **invalid/malformed JSON-LD**. This means:
- Google's Rich Results parser cannot read any structured data on these pages
- Zero eligibility for Article rich results, breadcrumbs, or any other markup on these pages
- The Article schemas list author and datePublished fields, but these are unreachable until the JSON syntax is fixed

**Action:** Validate all blog page schemas using Google's Rich Results Test (search.google.com/test/rich-results) and fix the JSON syntax errors. Common causes include unescaped quotes in description strings, trailing commas, and malformed `@context` declarations.

#### Organization sameAs — 13 Empty Strings

🔴 **Critical.** The global Organization schema declares 13 social media profile slots in the `sameAs` array, but every single entry is an empty string `""`. This schema is injected sitewide, meaning **every page on the site carries malformed structured data**.

```json
"sameAs": ["", "", "", "", "", "", "", "", "", "", "", "", ""]
```

This signals to Google that the organisation's Knowledge Graph entity has no social presence and may undermine Google's confidence in the brand's legitimacy. Either populate these with real URLs or remove the empty entries entirely.

#### Duplicate Product Schema

🟠 **High.** Every product page renders the Product JSON-LD block **twice** — two identical `<script type="application/ld+json">` blocks. This is almost certainly a theme rendering bug. While Google typically handles duplicate schemas gracefully, it creates unnecessary payload and increases the risk of conflicting data if the two blocks ever diverge.

#### priceValidUntil Expiring Imminently

🟠 **High.** All product schemas include:

```json
"priceValidUntil": "2026-03-27"
```

This date is **7 days from the audit date**. Once this date passes, Google may suppress Product rich results (price display in SERPs) for all product pages simultaneously. This requires an immediate update — set to at least 12 months in the future and implement a process to keep it updated.

### 5.2 Missing Schema Types

| Schema Type | Where Needed | Business Impact | Severity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LocalBusiness** | `/pages/showroom-22-sin-ming-lane` | Enables Google Maps integration, local pack eligibility, "visit in-store" rich result features | 🟠 High |
| **FAQPage** | Homepage (5 Q&As present) | FAQ rich results increase SERP space by 3–5×; directly targets consideration-stage queries | 🟠 High |
| **ContactPoint** | `/pages/mattress-delivery---contact` | Improves Knowledge Panel completeness | 🟡 Medium |
| **hreflang** (not schema, but paired concern) | All pages | Enables correct locale targeting for Middle East expansion | 🟠 High |

### 5.3 Incomplete Schema

| Issue | Detail | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| Article schema missing `author` and `datePublished` | Fields exist in template but are unreachable due to parse errors | 🟠 High (fix parse errors first) |

## 6. Performance

**Score: 52 / 100** *(estimated — direct CWV measurement not available for this audit; estimate based on Shopline platform benchmarks and page composition)*

### 6.1 Platform Context

Owllight runs on Shopline, a platform architecturally similar to Shopify. Shopline-hosted stores typically score in the 45–65 range on Google PageSpeed Insights for mobile, with the primary bottlenecks being:

- Large, unoptimised image payloads (particularly impactful here given the high image counts per page — up to 93 images on the 2000TC bedding page)
- Third-party script loading (chat widgets, analytics, marketing pixels)
- Render-blocking resources in the `<head>`

### 6.2 Image Payload Risk

The image counts per product page are extremely high:

| Page | Total Images |
|---|---|
| Bedding 2000TC | 93 |
| 4D Topper | 89 |
| Bedding 1600TC | 85 |
| Butterfly Pillow | 83 |
| Tulip Mattress | 74 |

Pages with 80–93 images represent a significant LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) and TBT (Total Blocking Time) risk. Without verified WebP conversion and lazy loading, these pages will score poorly on Core Web Vitals.

### 6.3 Recommendations

- Audit the image pipeline: confirm all images are served as WebP or AVIF, with appropriate `srcset` values
- Implement aggressive lazy loading for below-the-fold product gallery images
- Consider pagination or carousel virtualisation for product image galleries exceeding 20–30 images
- Reduce third-party script blocking by deferring non-critical scripts
- Enable Shopline's built-in CDN caching for static assets

| 3D Back Care Topper | 48 | 76 | 63% |
| Butterfly Pillow | 51 | 83 | 61% |
| Warehouse Sale | 34 | 54 | 63% |

### 7.3 Consequences of Missing Alt Text

1. **Search engines cannot understand image content** — product images showing the mattress in context, foam layer cross-sections, certifications, and lifestyle photography are invisible to Google Image Search
2. **Zero eligibility for Google Image Search traffic** — a meaningful supplementary traffic channel for e-commerce sleep brands
3. **Accessibility failure** — visually impaired users using screen readers encounter blank descriptions for all product imagery
4. **AI Search signal loss** — multimodal AI search systems (Google SGE, Bing Copilot) rely on alt text to understand product images in context

### 7.4 Alt Text Writing Guidelines

For this category of products, alt text should:
- Describe the product name and key attribute: `alt="Owllight Tulip Hybrid Mattress - Queen Size, CertiPUR-US certified foam layers"`
- For lifestyle/room shots: `alt="Owllight Tulip mattress in a modern Singapore bedroom"`
- For certification badges: `alt="CertiPUR-US certified foam certification badge"`
- For cross-section diagrams: `alt="Tulip mattress 5-zone pro spine support foam layer diagram"`
- Avoid keyword stuffing — write for a human who cannot see the image

## 8. AI Search Readiness

**Score: 32 / 100**

AI-powered search (Google AI Overviews, Bing Copilot, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity) is an increasingly significant traffic source for product-category and comparison queries — precisely the queries that Owllight's comparison blog posts and product pages are targeting. The site's current state makes it a poor candidate for AI search citation.

### 8.1 Key AI Search Deficiencies

| Factor | Current State | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Structured data validity | Broken on all blog pages, malformed sitewide (empty sameAs) | AI parsers rely on structured data for entity understanding |
| Brand entity clarity | No populated social profiles, no About page (redirects to homepage) | AI search systems cannot build a confident entity profile for Owllight |
| FAQ structured data | 5 Q&As on homepage, no FAQPage schema | Direct answer queries cannot be served from Owllight's content |
| Author/expertise signals | Article schemas missing author; no author bio pages visible | E-E-A-T signals absent — reduces citation likelihood for health/sleep advice content |
| Image understanding | ~65% of images lack alt text | Multimodal AI cannot understand product imagery |
| `/search` blocked for non-Google bots | AI crawlers (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot) may be affected by the `robots.txt` disallow | Reduces crawl coverage for AI indexing |
| Content depth on key pages | Collection pages, policy pages, and several products are very thin | AI systems prefer citing comprehensive, authoritative content |

### 8.2 AI Search Opportunities

Owllight's content is positioned well for AI search if the underlying issues are resolved:

- **Comparison blog posts** (King Koil, Sealy, Simmons, Woosa vs Owllight) directly answer the "best mattress Singapore" comparison queries that AI search systems frequently synthesise answers for. With valid Article schema and authoritative structure, these could become frequent AI citation sources.
- **Back care / orthopedic positioning** is a specific, differentiatable claim that AI systems can cite when answering "best mattress for back pain Singapore" queries — but only if the content has valid schema and sufficient depth.
- **FAQ content on homepage** should be marked up with FAQPage schema to be eligible for direct AI answer extraction.
- **Product specs** (firmness levels, certifications, dimensions, trial periods) should be structured consistently across all product pages — ideally with tabular data — to make AI extraction reliable.

### 8.3 robots.txt Recommendation for AI Crawlers

Review the current `robots.txt` to ensure no unintentional blocking of AI search crawlers. Key bots to check:
- `GPTBot` (ChatGPT / OpenAI)
- `PerplexityBot`
- `ClaudeBot` (Anthropic)
- `Google-Extended`
- `Applebot-Extended`

Unless there is a specific business reason to block these, they should be permitted to crawl product, blog, and category pages.
